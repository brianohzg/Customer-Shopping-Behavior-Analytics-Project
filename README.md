# Customer Shopping Behavior Analytics

## Overview
This project analyzes customer shopping behavior to uncover insights that drive business decisions.  
It demonstrates technical competency, business impact, and communication skills through a complete analytics workflow:
- Data loading and cleaning in Python
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- SQL queries on PostgreSQL for business questions
- Interactive dashboard in Power BI
- Business report and recommendations
- Presentation slides created with Gamma AI

---

## Dataset
- **File:** [Dataset_customer_shopping_behavior.csv](Dataset_customer_shopping_behavior.csv)  
- **Description:** Public dataset containing 3900 customer transactions, demographics, and shopping patterns.  
- Used for EDA, SQL queries, and dashboard visualizations.

## Steps
1. **Data Preparation**  
   - Load dataset in Python  
   - Handled missing values, normalize fields, remove redundancies and clean data

2. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**  
   - Visualize customer demographics and shopping trends  

3. **Data Analysis using SQL to answer Business Queries**  
   - File: [PostgreSQL_Customer Shopping Behavior.sql](PostgreSQL_Customer%20Shopping%20Behavior.sql)  
   - Business questions:
     - What is the total revenue generated by male vs. female customers?  
     - Which customers used a discount but still spent more than the average purchase amount?
     - Which are the top 10 products with the highest average review rating?
     - Compare the average Purchase Amounts between Standard and Express Shipping.
     - Do subscribed customers spend more? Compare average spend and total revenue between subscribers and non-subscribers.
     - Which 5 products have the highest percentage of purchases with discounts applied?
     - Segment customers into New, Returning, and Loyal based on their total number of previous purchases, and show the count of each segment.
     - Refine the segmentation rules to include Recency (purchase_frequency_days) with the frequency (previous_purchases), and show the customer count of each segment.
     - Which segments drive recurring revenue? Breakdown the counts and subscription rates (Subscription Status = 'Yes') across our customer segments.
     - What are the top 3 most purchased products within each category?
     - Are customers who are repeat buyers (more than 5 previous purchases) also likely to subscribe?
     - What is the revenue contribution of each age group?
     - Which payment method is associated with the highest purchase amount?

## Results & Recommendations
- **Key Insights:**
  - Males generate 2X more revenue than females 
  - No distinct differences for average purchase amount between Standard and Express shipping type 
  - Top 10 products with highest average review ratings
  - Top 5 discount dependant products
  - Top 3 products per category
  - Non subscribed customers are 2.7x times more than subscribers and generate 2.7x times the revenue
  - Young Adults are top revenue contributing age group
  - Repeat buyers are not subscribers yet
  - 79% of cutomer base are "loyal" customers
  - Credit Card drove the highest purchase amount

- **Business Recommendations:**
  - Focus marketing on weekend campaigns  
  - Expand bundled offers to increase basket size  
  - Target seasonal promotions to younger segments
